---
title: "Generating and reading your leaderboard"
description: "Generate the final leaderboard once you have scored participants, understand the ranking logic, and use read-only reports as your permanent record of results."
---

The leaderboard is the final step in the AI Jury workflow. It ranks participants by your Jury Scores — weighted by the criteria you set in the wizard — and freezes the project into a permanent, read-only record. Generating the leaderboard is irreversible, so it is worth scoring all participants before you proceed.

## When you can generate the leaderboard

The **Create Leaderboard** button becomes available once the project reaches `reviewing` status, which happens automatically when AI judging completes. You do not need to wait for all participants to be scored — but doing so gives you a complete ranking.

<Tip>
  Score all participants before generating the leaderboard. Once you confirm, all scoring inputs become read-only and cannot be changed.
</Tip>

## Generating the leaderboard

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Jury Review list">
    From your dashboard or the project header, navigate to the **Jury Review** list. This shows every participant with their AI Mode Score and current scoring status.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Create Leaderboard">
    When you are ready to finalize, click **Create Leaderboard**. A confirmation dialog appears.

    If any participants do not yet have a submitted Jury Score, a warning appears listing the unscored entries. You can go back and score them, or proceed anyway — there is no hard block.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm finalization">
    Confirm in the dialog to generate the leaderboard. The project moves to `scored` status. All scoring inputs become read-only immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Finalizing the leaderboard is permanent. All Jury Scores, AI scores, and reports are locked. There is no unlock or edit flow after this point.
</Warning>

## Reading the leaderboard

Participants are ranked from highest to lowest by their weighted Jury Score, calculated using the criteria weights you configured in step 3 of the wizard.

**Leaderboard columns:**

| Column | What it shows |
|---|---|
| Rank | Position based on weighted Jury Score |
| Participant name | Submitted participant name |
| Project name | Submitted project name |
| Final score | Weighted Jury Score — the ranking basis |
| AI Mode Score | The AI's weighted score — shown for reference, visually subdued |

A summary block at the top of the leaderboard shows total participant count, the average Jury Score, and score distribution across the field.

Click any row to open that participant's report in read-only mode. The report header shows **Finalized · \<date\>** to confirm the record is locked.

<Note>
  The leaderboard ranks by Jury Score, not by AI Score. The AI analysis informs your evaluation — it surfaces strengths, weaknesses, and questions you might not have caught on your own — but every position in the ranking reflects your human judgment.
</Note>

## After finalization

Once the project is in `scored` status:

- All participant reports are read-only. No scores, comments, or answer notes can be modified.
- AI scores remain visible in every report as a reference alongside the final Jury Scores.
- The leaderboard is the permanent record of results for the project.
- From the dashboard, clicking the project card opens the leaderboard directly.
