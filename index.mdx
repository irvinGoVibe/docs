AI Jury is a decision-support platform built for organizers of pitch competitions, hackathons, and accelerator programs. You set up a project, collect pitch decks from participants, and let a panel of specialized AI judges evaluate each submission. You then review the AI analysis, conduct live Q&A, set your own final scores, and publish a leaderboard — all in one place.

## How AI Jury works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a project">
    Use the five-step wizard to choose an evaluation mode (Pitch Competition, Hackathon, or Custom), set your criteria and weights, and configure how participants will submit their materials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Collect submissions">
    Either add participants manually or share a public link so participants upload their own pitch decks. AI Jury supports PDF, PPTX, and Google Slides.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run AI judging">
    Click **Start Judging**. A panel of specialized AI judges evaluates every submission in parallel, producing per-dimension scores, strengths, weaknesses, red flags, and suggested questions for your live Q&A.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and finalize">
    Open each participant's report, use the AI analysis during your live Q&A session, and enter your own Jury Scores. The leaderboard ranks participants by your scores — not the AI's.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  AI Jury's AI scores are advisory — they help you evaluate faster and more consistently, but every final score and ranking decision stays with you.
</Note>
