---
title: "Quickstart: run your first AI Jury project end-to-end"
description: "Create a project, add a participant, run AI judging, review the report, set your Jury Scores, and publish your first leaderboard in one session."
---

This guide walks you through a complete AI Jury workflow — from creating an account to publishing a leaderboard. Follow the steps in order; each one picks up where the previous left off.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign up or sign in">
    Go to AI Jury and create an account or sign in.

    You can sign up with an **email and password** or continue with **Google**. If you use email, check your inbox for a verification link and click it before proceeding — your account isn't active until you verify.

    After signing in, you land on the **Dashboard**, which lists all your projects.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a project">
    Click **Create project** in the dashboard header. This opens the five-step project wizard.

    Work through each step in order:

    | Step | What you configure |
    |---|---|
    | **1. Mode** | Choose your evaluation format. Select **Pitch Competition** — this is the primary mode in the current version, with a six-judge panel covering dimensions P1–P6. |
    | **2. Details** | Enter a project name, description, start and end dates, and timezone. |
    | **3. Criteria** | Set the scoring criteria and their weights. Weights must add up to 100%. The **Next** button stays disabled until they do. |
    | **4. Judges** | Review the preset Pitch Competition judge panel. The six judges and their dimension assignments are pre-configured. You can adjust dimension weights if needed. |
    | **5. Participants** | Choose how you'll collect submissions. Select **Manual** to add participants yourself, or **Self-upload** to generate a public link participants can use to submit their own materials. |

    Completing step 5 saves the project and opens **Project Overview**.

    <Tip>
      The wizard auto-saves after every change. If you close it before finishing, click the project card on the Dashboard to pick up where you left off.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add your first participant">
    On the Project Overview page, click **Add participant**. A modal form opens.

    Fill in the required fields:

    - **Participant name** — the person's full name
    - **Project name** — the name of their startup or project
    - **Pitch deck** — upload a PDF or PPTX file, or paste a Google Slides URL

    You can also attach an optional video link, repository URL, or a comment for your own reference.

    Click **Save**. The participant appears in the table. If you attached a pitch deck, their status shows as **Ready** — meaning they're eligible for AI judging.

    <Note>
      A participant must have a pitch deck (uploaded file or Google Slides URL) to be judged. Participants without one show as **Pending** and are skipped when judging runs.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Run AI judging">
    Once at least one participant has **Ready** status, the **Start Judging** button becomes active. Click it.

    A progress modal opens and the project moves into **judging** status. The pipeline runs each submission through four stages:

    1. **Decoder** — extracts and normalizes text from the pitch deck (PDF, PPTX, or Google Slides)
    2. **AI Judges** — six specialized judges evaluate the submission in parallel across dimensions P1–P6
    3. **Summarizer** — aggregates the six judge outputs into a unified report structure
    4. **Scoring** — calculates per-dimension scores, confidence-adjusted aggregates, and spread labels (Consensus / Split / Conflict)

    You can close the modal without stopping the pipeline — judging continues in the background. Reopen the modal at any time to check progress.

    When all submissions finish processing, the project moves to **reviewing** status.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review the participant report">
    From Project Overview, click a participant's name to open their report. The report opens on the **Executive Summary** tab by default.

    The report has five tabs:

    | Tab | What it shows |
    |---|---|
    | **Executive Summary** | AI scores for P1–P6 with confidence labels, a project summary, strengths and weaknesses with consensus markers, and reasons why the project might or might not advance |
    | **Jury Preparation** | The scoring matrix with AI scores as reference, plus a block of suggested questions for your live Q&A session |
    | **Scores** | Methodology overview, per-dimension score breakdowns, and the judge contribution matrix with spread indicators |
    | **Judges** | Six accordion cards — one per AI judge — with each judge's primary concern, suggested live questions, scores, top signals, and raw reasoning |
    | **Reviewer** | A duplicate of the Jury Preparation scoring matrix, kept in sync in real time |

    Read through the Executive Summary and Judges tabs to understand how the AI analyzed this participant before you score them.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set your Jury Score">
    Click the **Jury Preparation** tab. This is where you enter your final scores.

    The scoring matrix shows each dimension (P1–P6) with:
    - The AI's suggested score as a read-only reference
    - A slider (0–10, in 0.1 increments) for your Jury Score
    - A live delta indicator showing the difference between your score and the AI's

    Drag each slider to the score you want to assign. Use the **+** and **−** buttons for precise adjustments. You can also add an optional comment per dimension.

    When you're satisfied, click **Save**. Your scores are saved as a draft immediately. You can return and adjust them any time before you generate the leaderboard.

    <Tip>
      You don't have to score every dimension in one sitting. A counter shows how many of the six dimensions you've filled in. Come back to finish later if needed.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate the leaderboard">
    After scoring participants, return to Project Overview. When you're ready to finalize results, click **Generate leaderboard** (or the equivalent button in your project view).

    A confirmation dialog appears. Confirm to:

    - Lock all Jury Scores as read-only
    - Move the project to **Scored** status
    - Rank participants by their Jury Score, weighted by the criteria you set in the wizard

    The leaderboard shows each participant's rank, name, project name, final Jury Score, and their AI Mode Score for reference. AI scores appear in a lighter style — they're informational, not the ranking basis.

    <Note>
      If some participants don't have submitted Jury Scores yet, you'll see a warning before generating. You can still proceed — the system won't hard-block you — but unscored participants won't rank correctly.
    </Note>

    Participant reports remain accessible as read-only after the leaderboard is created, preserving the full audit trail between AI analysis and your final decisions.
  </Step>
</Steps>
